About Morphology Codes

Morphology codes provide grammatical information about words in the Biblical text. They include details like:

  • Part of speech (noun, verb, adjective, etc.)
  • Tense, voice, and mood for verbs
  • Case, number, and gender for nouns
  • Other grammatical features

The STEPBible system uses standardized codes for both Hebrew and Greek texts.

Hebrew Morphology

Hebrew morphology codes describe the grammatical features of words in the Hebrew Bible (Old Testament).

Common Hebrew Codes:
  • A - Adjective
  • C - Conjunction
  • D - Adverb
  • N - Noun
  • P - Pronoun
  • R - Preposition
  • T - Particle
  • V - Verb
Greek Morphology

Greek morphology codes describe the grammatical features of words in the Greek New Testament.

Common Greek Codes:
  • A - Adjective
  • C - Conjunction
  • D - Adverb
  • N - Noun
  • P - Pronoun
  • R - Preposition
  • T - Particle
  • V - Verb
